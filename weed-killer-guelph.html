Can You Apply Weed Killer in Guelph? Ontario's Rules Explained

April 2026 • 4 min read

This is one of the most common questions we get. The short answer: some weed control is still legal in Guelph, but it's more restricted than most homeowners realize.

Ontario's Cosmetic Pesticides Ban

Ontario banned the use of pesticides for cosmetic purposes on lawns and gardens under the Pesticides Act amendments that took effect in 2009. What's prohibited for residential lawn use includes most broadleaf herbicides like 2,4-D.

What IS Still Allowed

  • Severe infestations: Licensed IPM practitioners can apply approved products when there is a significant infestation that cultural practices can't control
  • Certain active ingredients: Some lower-risk products (iron-based herbicides, fatty acid herbicides) are on the permitted list and can be used by licensed applicators

What This Means Practically

We are licensed pesticide applicators in Ontario. For most lawns, the honest answer is: cultural practices outperform anything we could spray anyway.

  • Mowing at 3-4 inches (tall grass shades out weed seedlings)
  • Fall fertilization (root energy for spring density)
  • Annual core aeration + overseeding (fills thin spots before weeds can)
  • Proper watering (deep and infrequent, not shallow and frequent)

The Most Common Weeds in Guelph Lawns

  • Dandelions: Most effectively managed by hand removal or iron-based spot treatments.
  • Crabgrass: Annual grass weed that germinates in late spring. Corn gluten meal is a permitted organic option.
  • Creeping Charlie: Thrives in shaded, moist areas. Shade-tolerant grass varieties help more than herbicides.
  • White clover: Often a sign of low nitrogen. A good fertilization program reduces clover by thickening the grass.

The Bottom Line

Yes, some weed control is still legal in Guelph. But the most effective approach to a weed-free lawn is a proper cultural program: right mowing height, seasonal fertilization, and aeration + overseeding every 1-2 years.
